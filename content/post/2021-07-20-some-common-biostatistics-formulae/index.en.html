1. Risk

Risk indicates the likelihood of an event occurring. This is similar to the concepts of proportion and probability.

\[ \text{Risk of an event } \mathcal{A}= \text{The probability or the likelihood that the event $\mathcal{A}$ will occur } = \frac{\text{number of outcome of event }\mathcal{A}}{\text{total number of outcomes}} \]

\[ \pi = \frac{n(\mathcal{A})}{\mathcal{N}} \]

40 out of 1000 children have jaundice.

\[ risk ~of~ children~ having~ jaundice= \frac{40}{1000} = 0.04 \]

This means that 4% of the children suffer from jaundice.

Observe that the formulae of risk, likelihood and probability are similar.

2. Relative Risk

The relative risk of an event is a comparison of two risks of the event at two different situations of another event. The relative risk, \(\rho\) of the success of an event A (1 denotes success) at two levels (1 and 2) of another event B,

\[ \rho = \frac{P(A=1|B=1)}{P(A=1|B=2)} \]

The denominator of the term above is the baseline risk. It is the risk with which the comparison is being made.
